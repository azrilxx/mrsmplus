Chapter 5: Respiration in Living Organisms

What is the primary gas required for cellular respiration?
a) Carbon dioxide
b) Oxygen
c) Nitrogen
d) Hydrogen
Answer: b

Explain the difference between aerobic and anaerobic respiration.
Answer: Aerobic respiration requires oxygen and produces more ATP (38 molecules), while anaerobic respiration occurs without oxygen and produces less ATP (2 molecules).

True or False: Plants only carry out photosynthesis and do not respire.
Answer: False

Fill in the blank: The process of respiration occurs in the _______ of the cell.
Answer: mitochondria

Which of the following is a product of cellular respiration?
a) Glucose
b) Oxygen
c) Carbon dioxide
d) Sunlight
Answer: c

The energy currency of the cell is _______.
Answer: ATP

During respiration, glucose is broken down to release _______.
Answer: energy

What happens to the rate of respiration when temperature increases within normal limits?
Answer: The rate of respiration increases as enzymes work more efficiently at higher temperatures.

Multiple Choice: What is the chemical equation for cellular respiration?
a) C6H12O6 + 6O2 → 6CO2 + 6H2O + ATP
b) 6CO2 + 6H2O → C6H12O6 + 6O2
c) 2H2O → 2H2 + O2
d) NaCl → Na+ + Cl-
Answer: a

Short Answer: Why do we breathe faster during exercise?
Answer: During exercise, our muscles need more energy, so we breathe faster to supply more oxygen for cellular respiration and to remove excess carbon dioxide.

Chapter 6: Life Processes - Nutrition

What type of nutrition do green plants exhibit?
a) Heterotrophic
b) Autotrophic
c) Parasitic
d) Saprophytic
Answer: b

True or False: Chlorophyll is essential for photosynthesis.
Answer: True

The opening and closing of stomata is controlled by _______ cells.
Answer: guard

Describe the role of the small intestine in digestion.
Answer: The small intestine is where most digestion and absorption occurs. It has villi that increase surface area for better absorption of nutrients into the bloodstream.

Which enzyme breaks down proteins?
a) Amylase
b) Lipase
c) Pepsin
d) Catalase
Answer: c
